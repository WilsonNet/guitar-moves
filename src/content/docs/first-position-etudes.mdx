## Introduction

**First position** on the guitar encompasses all the notes available in the first four frets, plus the open strings. This is where every guitarist begins their journey.

In this section, you'll find progressive etudes to build your foundation:
1. Open string exercises for right-hand technique
2. Natural notes in first position
3. Simple melodies
4. Basic scale patterns

---

## Practice Tips

1. **Posture:** Sit with the guitar on your left leg (classical position) or right leg (casual). Keep your back straight.

2. **Left Hand:** Keep fingers curved, fingertips pressing down just behind the frets. Thumb should be positioned behind the neck, roughly opposite the middle finger.

3. **Right Hand:** For these exercises, use free stroke ( Tirando ) for a lighter sound, or rest stroke ( Apoyando ) for a fuller, more pronounced tone.

4. **Timing:** Practice with a metronome. Speed will come naturally from accuracy.

5. **Daily Routine:** Spend 5-10 minutes on these exercises as part of your warm-up.

---

## Next Steps

Once these etudes feel comfortable:
- Learn the G major scale (introduces F# on the 1st string, 2nd fret)
- Practice position shifts to 2nd and 3rd position
- Explore simple chord progressions using these notes

*"Slow practice is fast progress."* — Classical Guitar Wisdom
